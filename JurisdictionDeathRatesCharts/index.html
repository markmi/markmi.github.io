Charts follow of: 2015+ Jurisdiction Death Rates History (All Causes) for the COVID-19 time frame and before, mostly for large populations, and for NYC and Arizona, Rate being fraction of jurisdiction's Age Range population dying (WeeklyMean rates over 4 week intervals, other than when 2020's MMWR 53 is involved), fraction expressed as N per Million (10000 <-> 1%). There are only 6 Age Range groups displayed here (top to bottom): 85..99+, 75..84, 65..74, 45..64, 25..44, 00..24.

CDC data update used: 2022-Apr-13 (publishing date, data is somewhat older).
Note: The more recent weeks charted are more likely to still have significantly incomplete data.

Note: The jurisdiction of each death here is the place-of-death jurisdiction, not the jurisdiction-of-residency one. But the fraction-of-population figures are based on residency-population figures. So, for example, the NYC population fractions are more ballpark-figures than they would be for counts of a classification directly tied to just NYC residency. I've not found regularly published place-of-residency death figures.
